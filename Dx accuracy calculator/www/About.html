This is the first of a collection of interactive Shiny apps that allow you to calculate diagnostic accuracy statistics and explore the intricacies of evaluating and using diagnostic tests:
  •  Diagnostic accuracy calculator.  App to calculate the main diagnostic accuracy statistics.
  • Sample size planner. Interactive app for estimating sample sizes for diagnostic accuracy studies.
  • Interpreting diagnostic accuracy measures. Interactive app to explore the main measures of diagnostic accuracy: sensitivity and specificity (technically useful) and positive and negative predictive values (clinically useful )  (under construction).
  • Interpreting ROC curves. Interactive app to explore the trade offs between high sensitivity and high specificity when selecting the classification threshold for a test with a continuous result. (under construction).
  • Interpreting the clinical utility of diagnostic tests. Interactive app to explore how to take account of the uncertainties in test results and the values placed on the range of possible outcomes (benefits, harms, costs) when making clinical decisions. (under construction).
  • Interpreting evaluations with an imperfect reference test. cto explore the uncertainties that arise in evaluating a diagnostic test when there is no gold standard to compare its performance. (under construction).
  • Interpreting epidemic transmissibility measures. Interactive app to explore the effects of the infection's transmissibility and the test's accuracy on the course of an epidemic. (under construction).

You can use this app to:
  • Calculate diagnostic accuracy measures
  • See how predictive values vary with prevalence
  • See the uncertainties in positive and negative predictive values

See how predictive values vary with prevalence

The main reason for developing this app was to make it obvious how positive and negative predictive values vary with prevalence.
This is crucially important to understanding:
  • How the design of a diagnostic accuracy study (cohort or case-control) determines whether or not it can estimate the predictive values of a test result
  • How the prevalence of a condition determines the predictive values of a test, which is particularly important when a test is to be used to screen for a condition when the prevalence is low, or to exclude a condition when the prevalence is high

Tip: Limitations of case-control studies
A cohort design allows the sensitivity and specificity, positive and negative predictive values, prevalence, and study size to be calculated from (TP, FP, FN, TN).

In contrast, a case-control design allows only sensitivity and specificity to be calculated.

Because of these constraints, the calculator requires you to first identify the study design.

The calculator then needs to be told the type of data that will be entered:

  • Either TP, FP, TN, FN
  • Or sensitivity and specificity

If the study design is case-control (or unknown), the calculator requires estimates of the prevalence and the study size. These are also required if you are using sensitivity and specificity data to calculate predictive values.

The calculator does not expect you to remember what information you need to provide: it knows what it needs, and asks for it.

Tip: use the zoom button to see detail at the ends of the graph
The zoom button on the graph of predictive value against prevalence is useful for looking the PPV curve at low prevalences (close to zero) and the NPV curve at high prevalences (close to one).
The zoom button is normally hidden. But, if you move your mouse's cursor over the graph, a line of little action buttons will appear above the graph. Experiment with the buttons to see how they work.

See the uncertainties in positive and negative predictive values

The third purpose of the calculator is to visuaslize the statistical uncertainty in predictive values, and how increasing the sample size of the study increases the precision of the predictive values. Understanding this source of uncertainty can inform clinical decision making and the planning of diagnostic evaluation studies. However, if you are planning a diagnostic accuracy study, you should check out our app for estimating study sample size.
